Aged to Perfection

A minimum of 12 months, often 24 or more, allowing complex flavors and a granular texture to develop beautifully.

Nutrient Rich

Exquisite Flavor

Our Story: Centuries of Tradition

The history of Parmigiano Reggiano stretches back over nine centuries to the Middle Ages. It was first produced by Benedictine and Cistercian monks in the areas of Parma and Reggio Emilia, who sought a way to preserve milk.

The secret lies in its natural production process, using only milk from local cows, rennet, and salt, without any additives. Every wheel is crafted by hand, following stringent rules set by the Parmigiano Reggiano Consortium, ensuring its unparalleled quality and authenticity.

From the lush pastures where the cows graze to the skilled hands of the cheesemakers and the long, patient aging in silent warehouses, each step is a testament to dedication and respect for tradition. This commitment has earned it the title of "King of Cheeses" worldwide.
