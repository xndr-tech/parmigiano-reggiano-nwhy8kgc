Why Parmigiano Reggiano Stands Apart

The Story Behind the Flavor

A Tradition Spanning Nine Centuries

Parmigiano Reggiano is more than just cheese; it's a testament to centuries of artisanal dedication. Originating in the Middle Ages from the Benedictine and Cistercian monasteries of Emilia-Romagna, its production methods have remained largely unchanged. Each wheel represents an unwavering commitment to quality, tradition, and the unique terroir of its birthplace.

The Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status ensures that only cheese produced in the provinces of Parma, Reggio Emilia, Modena, Bologna (on the left bank of the Reno river), and Mantua (on the right bank of the Po river) using stringent methods can bear the name Parmigiano Reggiano. This guarantees its authenticity and superior taste.

How to Savor Parmigiano Reggiano

Grate Over Pasta

The classic application: freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano elevates any pasta dish, risotto, or soup with its umami-rich flavor.

Shave onto Salads

Delicate shavings add a sophisticated touch and a burst of flavor to fresh salads, carpaccios, or vegetable dishes.

Enjoy as a Snack

Break off irregular chunks and enjoy with a glass of robust red wine, balsamic vinegar, or fresh fruit for a true Italian experience.

In Culinary Creations

Incorporate it into sauces, pestos, omelets, or bake into savory pastries for an irresistible depth of flavor.

Pair with Honey or Jam

Discover a delightful contrast by pairing Parmigiano Reggiano with fig jam, chestnut honey, or other sweet preserves.

Broth Infusion

Don't discard the rind! Simmer it in soups or broths to impart a rich, savory depth that enhances any liquid base.
